Project TRACE — How it works, end to end

This document serves as the complete operational manual for Project TRACE. It explains how the system functions in plain language, mapping the exact digital workflow to the physical realities of the PLP Registrar's Office.

Core Operational Principles

Automation over Manual Entry

Clerks are decision-makers, not data encoders. The system uses artificial intelligence to read uploaded documents and automatically fill out forms, eliminating typos and speeding up intake.

Seamless Handoffs

Documents travel instantly. When Window 1 uploads a file, it immediately lands on the College Secretary's screen for approval, without anyone leaving their desk.

Student Peace of Mind

Students no longer need to line up at the registrar window just to ask "is my paper ready?" They receive automatic SMS updates and a live tracking page.

Streamlined Workflow

We built exactly what the office needs. No bloated features—just a highly optimized pipeline moving from Window 1 to the Secretary and back.

1. What powers TRACE (The Technology)

To ensure speed, security, and intelligence, TRACE is built using a modern software stack. Here is what runs under the hood:

  • The Screens (React & Tailwind): We use React to build fast, app-like websites. When a clerk clicks "Approve", the screen updates instantly without reloading. Tailwind makes the design look clean and modern.
  • The Engine (Node.js & Express): This is the server that runs 24/7. It safely receives files uploaded by the clerks and ensures everything is saved securely.
  • The Brain (Python & EasyOCR): Instead of basic text readers, we use a Deep Learning AI model (built on PyTorch) to read messy, handwritten, or typed documents like a human eye.
  • The Memory (MySQL): The central database that remembers every student's details, exactly where every document is, and when it was approved.
  • The Traffic Cop (n8n): An automation engine that looks at a document and instantly moves it to the correct person's computer screen based on the university's rules.

2. User Roles & Responsibilities

TRACE is a collaborative ecosystem. Every user has a specific view tailored to what they need to see.

RoleWho They AreWhat They Do in TRACE
The Student Current students or alumni of PLP. Logs into a simple website. They submit requests for credentials, upload requirements, and monitor their document's progress.
Window 1 (Intake) The front desk clerk. Uploads physical papers handed to them. Releases finished documents to students.
College Secretary The approver. Reviews the digital files in their dashboard, verifies the information, and clicks "Approve" or "Return."

3. Feature 1: The Student Request Portal

This is the starting point of the document journey. It is designed to be as simple as ordering an item online.

  • Step 0: Account Verification: Students sign up on the portal and upload a picture of their Student ID or Diploma. The Python AI engine will attempt to auto-verify the account instantly. If the ID is too blurry, it is flagged as "pending" for manual admin review.
  • Step 1: Intake & Payment: A verified student logs into the portal and uploads their document request. They must immediately pay via GCash/QRPh. The system holds the document until payment is verified.
  • Live Tracking: By clicking the link in the text message, the student sees a timeline. Instead of a vague "Pending" status, it shows exact stages: "Received by Window 1" → "Under Evaluation by Secretary" → "Ready for Pickup."

4. Feature 2: The Clerk Workspace

Traditional physical logbooks are replaced by a clean, digital "To-Do List."

  • The Focused Queue: The College Secretary does not see a massive list of every document in the school. They only see the documents currently awaiting their approval.
  • One-Click Actions: Once the Secretary verifies the document is correct, they simply click a green "Approve" button.

5. Feature 3: Smart Data Extraction (No Typing)

Data entry is the slowest part of administrative work. TRACE utilizes Artificial Intelligence (Computer Vision) to do the heavy lifting.

  • Reading the Image: When Window 1 uploads a photo of a student's form, the AI scans the image.
  • Auto-Filling: It isolates the Student Number and Form Type, and automatically types it into the digital database. When the Secretary opens the file, all the student's details are already filled out. The Secretary only needs to double-check it.

6. Feature 4: Automated Desk Routing

TRACE uses an automation engine (n8n) to handle the hand-offs instantly.

  • The Core Loop: When Window 1 uploads the document, n8n instantly sends a digital copy to the Secretary's screen.
  • The Return Trip: When the Secretary clicks "Approve", n8n instantly pulls it off their screen, places it back on Window 1's screen, and texts the student that the document is ready for pickup.

Operations GuideProject Rollout & Implementation

Deploying Project TRACE requires a structured rollout to ensure operations are never disrupted.

  1. Phase 1: System Initialization & Silent Pilot

    Configure the system and run a quiet test without involving students.

    Operational Steps:

    • Database Configuration: Input the Window 1 Clerk and the College Secretary into the system.
    • The Silent Run: For one week, whenever Window 1 processes a physical paper, they also upload it into TRACE to ensure the AI and the digital hand-offs to the Secretary work perfectly.

  2. Phase 2: The Public Student Launch

    Open the digital gates to the student body.

    Operational Steps:

    • Campus Announcement: The university officially announces the TRACE Student Portal and the new cashless payment policy (GCash/QRPh).
    • Transitioning Walk-ins: Guide physical walk-in students to use the portal to submit their requirements and pay digitally, teaching them the new protocol.
    • Monitoring Webhooks & SMS: Verify that payment confirmation webhooks from the gateway are succeeding, and automated text messages containing tracking links are reaching student phones.

  3. Phase 3: Optimization

    With the system fully running, rely entirely on the AI Intake to streamline efficiency.

    Operational Steps:

    • Clerks officially stop manually typing student details into their old Excel sheets, relying completely on the Python EasyOCR engine.
    • The core loop (Window 1 ➔ Secretary ➔ Window 1) becomes the daily standard operating procedure.
